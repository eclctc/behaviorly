Career Trajectory

RBT is one of the most in-demand occupations in the country, growing at a rate of almost 10% from 2021 - 2031 according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. RBT pays above the national average for starting behavior health occupations, and offers several different paths forward for those looking to stay within the field.

Career Options
Beyond RBT

Many RBTs develop a passion for the field of Applied Behavior Analysis and choose to further their education and career by getting certified as a Board-Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA). BCBAs are responsible for developing treatment plans for the clients, supervising RBTs, providing parent training support, and monitoring ongoing changes in the client’s skills levels and challenging behaviors. Many BCBAs also choose to move into leadership positions in the field such as regional directors, center directors, training directors, mentors, BACB supervisors, quality assurance, or opening their own ABA companies.
