An RBT or Registered Behavior Technician is a paraprofessional-level service provider who works in a home, school, community, or clinic setting directly with individuals diagnosed with autism or other behavioral health conditions or developmental disabilities. They provide behavior analytic services under the supervision of a BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst).

The RBT credential is great for anyone interested in the field of behavior analysis or autism, including students pursuing psychology or education degrees, teachers, school paraprofessionals, and anyone interested in helping children with developmental disabilities learn the skills and behaviors required to increase their chance of independence and inclusion in school and community settings.

You can keep your RBT credential during times you are in between jobs or supervisors by applying for inactive status through your BACB account until regular supervision resumes.
